# Acumen report review notes

## Immediate verdict

The report is stronger than the exemplar in presentation, intellectual framing, and honesty about feasibility. The main risks are not polish. They are marker navigation, word count, and the evidence gap between the current UCL-heavy primary research and the later NextGen segment.

## Against the brief

The brief asks for the three essential questions plus three optional questions. The report clearly answers the essentials:

- Product/service: Sections 03 to 05.
- Customer need: Sections 02 and 07.
- Feasibility conclusion: Section 09.

It also answers more than three optional questions, but it should signpost the chosen three. The cleanest selection is:

- First customers and what they are like: Sections 02 and 07.
- Underlying technology: Section 05.
- IP protection and ownership: Section 05, "Honest limitations and IP".

Market size is present only indirectly through APAC wealth transfer and Year-1 revenue. If the team wants market size to be one of the chosen optional questions, add a compact TAM/SAM/SOM table. Otherwise, leave market size as supporting context and avoid implying it is one of the chosen three.

## Marking-risk fixes

1. Add a marker-facing line in the executive summary:

   "This report answers the three essential questions and focuses on three optional questions: first customers, underlying technology, and IP strategy. Market sizing is treated as supporting commercial context rather than a selected optional question."

2. Add one sentence before the Year-1 revenue table:

   "The Year-1 figure is deliberately modest because it models proof of willingness to pay rather than scale: 1,200 individual unlocks, six institutional cohorts, and one paid NextGen pilot."

3. Add one sentence under the pie charts:

   "Free-text responses were coded once by the team into mutually exclusive categories; no inter-rater reliability claim is made at this stage."

4. If word count is tight, cut prose before adding anything. Best cuts:

   - Shorten "Who Acumen is built for" by 60 to 80 words.
   - Remove one paragraph from "What the SWOT actually tells us".
   - Merge "Where Acumen actually competes" into the Porter table caption or one short paragraph.
   - Reduce "The argument in one line" to two sentences.

## Exemplar comparison

Acumen is more professionally presented than GoCash and has a clearer feasibility logic. GoCash was stronger on explicit market sizing and visual market-entry evidence, but weaker in writing quality, figure discipline, and analytical originality. Acumen's simulation framework, theory grounding, and honest limitation language are stronger.

The exemplar lesson to borrow is not the prose style. It is marker navigation: clear scope, clear selected tools, and obvious answer locations.

## Dash and tone pass

I removed visible em dashes from the recovered HTML copy. The remaining tone is mostly appropriate: professional, clear, and not overclaiming. Avoid adding phrases like "revolutionary", "transformative", "game-changing", or "AI-powered leadership intelligence"; the current cautious language is a strength.

## Word count warning

The brief states 3,000 words maximum with no extra allowance. Tables, figures, appendices, and bibliography are excluded. A local prose-only estimate from the original HTML before recovery was about 3,615 words, so the main body should be trimmed before submission unless your official count is lower.
