# Acumen Coursework Cohesion Plan

Working name: **Acumen**

Positioning line:
**Business judgment simulations for emerging leaders.**

Core argument:
Acumen helps future business leaders practise high-stakes business decisions, converts choices into a structured leadership signal, and uses that signal to create more meaningful peer matching.

## One-Sentence Venture Definition

Acumen is a simulation-based leadership readiness platform for students and young professionals, using APAC family-business succession, governance, and crisis decision-making as its research-backed scenario context.

## What Has Been Achieved

Strongly achieved:
- Clear product concept and MVP loop: simulate -> measure -> match.
- Working Next.js prototype with onboarding, dashboard, three scenarios, simulation flow, scoring, feedback, profile, and network pages.
- Scenario content aligned with the research: succession, governance, political/regulatory risk, legacy assets, stakeholder pressure, and cognitive bias.
- Academic framework grounding: Upper Echelons Theory, Vroom-Yetton, OODA, Rest's ethical model, Hersey-Blanchard, behavioural economics.
- Secondary market evidence: Indonesian/APAC family business scale, succession delays, senior resistance, low agility, AI hesitancy, and intergenerational wealth transfer.
- SWOT and Porter's Five Forces material already drafted in depth.
- Report screenshots captured in `report_screenshots/`.

Partially achieved:
- Primary research is planned but still thin. This is the main grading risk.
- Business Model Canvas and Value Proposition Canvas are referenced but need final polished diagrams.
- MVP limitations are known but need to be framed explicitly: no real CV parsing, no live map, no real matching algorithm, no scoring validation study.
- Market segment needs narrowing: start with students/young professionals for accessible validation, then position APAC NextGen family business leaders as the strategic beachhead.

Not yet achieved:
- Survey/interview evidence sufficient for top-band research marks.
- Validated willingness to pay.
- Full technical architecture diagram.
- Evidence-backed prioritisation of next steps.
- Exemplar-report comparison, because no exemplar PDF was found in the repo.

## Evidence Map

Research finding -> Product response:

APAC succession is high-stakes and delayed -> Succession Crisis scenario.
Senior resistance is common -> scenarios include founder shadow, authority, family council, board process.
Family firms rely on trust and loyalty -> stakeholder management scoring and peer matching.
Governance failures damage performance -> fraud allegation, independent committee, Fitch/lender pressure.
NextGen need readiness and legitimacy -> leadership profile and score report.
Serious games can assess soft skills contextually -> branching simulation instead of static quiz.
Behavioural assessment tracks strategy, not just right answers -> hidden scoring rubric and bias profile.
Networking platforms are shallow -> matches based on decision profile and complementary strengths.

## Compact SWOT Diagram Copy

Use a 2x2 diagram with one headline per quadrant and 3 bullets max.

Strengths:
- Research-backed simulation engine.
- Culturally specific APAC succession scenarios.
- Assessment plus peer matching creates a differentiated loop.

Weaknesses:
- Primary validation still limited.
- Scoring model not yet empirically calibrated.
- Network value depends on user density.

Opportunities:
- APAC intergenerational wealth transfer and succession pressure.
- University/family-business association pilot channels.
- B2B use by recruiters, advisors, and entrepreneurship centres.

Threats:
- Incumbents such as LinkedIn, Coursera, McKinsey-style assessment tools.
- Cultural sensitivity around private family governance.
- Trust risk if cognitive scores are perceived as unvalidated.

## Compact Porter Diagram Copy

Centre: Acumen, simulation-based leadership readiness platform.

Competitive rivalry: Low direct, moderate indirect.
No exact competitor combines APAC succession simulations, cognitive profiling, and peer matching. Indirect rivals include business schools, leadership courses, LinkedIn, Coursera, Harvard simulations, and mentoring networks.

Threat of new entrants: Moderate.
The interface is buildable, but cultural content, scoring credibility, and partner distribution are harder to copy.

Supplier power: Low-to-moderate.
Cloud and AI APIs are substitutable. Expert scenario authors and governance advisors are the real supplier risk.

Buyer power: Moderate B2C, high B2B.
Students have low switching costs and uncertain willingness to pay. Institutional buyers require validation, customisation, and proof of outcomes.

Threat of substitutes: High early, lower with scale.
Substitutes include MBAs, coaching, family councils, internships, LinkedIn, and learning-by-doing. Accumulated profiles, certificates, and peer networks reduce substitution risk over time.

## Primary Research Sprint

Minimum viable evidence:
- 10-20 survey responses.
- 3-5 short interviews.
- At least 2 target types: business students/young professionals and anyone with family-business exposure.

Must-answer survey outputs:
- % who feel underprepared for leadership decisions.
- % who would use a realistic simulation platform.
- % who value skill-based peer matching.
- Price sensitivity.
- What would make them trust the assessment.

Interview prompts to prioritise:
- Tell me about a difficult leadership/business decision you have faced or expect to face.
- What current tools help you practise that kind of judgment?
- Would a simulation score feel useful, credible, or gimmicky?
- What would make you trust or distrust the feedback?
- Would matching with complementary peers be valuable?

## Feasibility Verdict

Acumen is feasible as a web-first MVP and credible as a coursework venture concept. The strongest opportunity is not a generic leadership app, but a focused readiness platform for high-stakes, culturally specific business judgment. The main unresolved risk is not technical feasibility; it is customer validation and assessment credibility.
