# Acumen product loop and MVP walkthrough

## Core product loop

Use this as the simple diagram in the report:

**Simulate -> Signal -> Connect -> Improve**

1. **Simulate**
   The user chooses a short business scenario and makes decisions under pressure.

2. **Signal**
   Acumen turns those decisions into a leadership profile, showing strengths, risks, and decision tendencies.

3. **Connect**
   The user sees peers whose skills or perspectives complement their own profile.

4. **Improve**
   The user repeats new scenarios, compares progress, and builds a clearer sense of their business judgement.

Short caption:

**Acumen creates a repeatable loop where users practise judgement, receive a clear signal, connect with complementary peers, and return to improve through new scenarios.**

## MVP user scenario

Use a scenario walkthrough in the report, not a collage.

A collage looks like design evidence. A walkthrough looks like product evidence. The marker should be able to understand the customer problem, solution, and value proposition in one sequence.

Recommended walkthrough:

1. **Landing page**
   A business student or early-career professional opens Acumen because they want to practise business judgement before facing real work, interviews, founder decisions, or family-business responsibility.

2. **Onboarding**
   The user selects interests and skill priorities. This frames Acumen as personal development, not a generic quiz app.

3. **Scenario choice**
   The user chooses a realistic business simulation. The current MVP can show startup, governance, or succession-style scenarios, but the report should explain that APAC family-business content is the research-backed advanced pathway.

4. **Decision simulation**
   The user makes trade-off decisions across strategy, ethics, stakeholder pressure, and communication.

5. **Feedback**
   The user receives a research-informed leadership profile. This is not described as a validated psychometric score at MVP stage.

6. **Network**
   The user sees suggested peers with complementary skills, turning the experience from private self-assessment into a social learning loop.

7. **Return**
   The user repeats simulations to improve their judgement profile and build a stronger professional identity.

## How the BMC and value proposition should be reconciled

The BMC is mostly aimed at a broad beachhead:

**Business students, young professionals, and aspiring founders who want sharper business judgement and better professional connections.**

The value proposition canvas is more focused on the premium later segment:

**NextGen family-business successors who need readiness, confidence, and credible leadership signals before formal succession.**

This is not a problem if the report explains the sequence clearly:

**Beachhead first. Premium segment later.**

Acumen should launch with accessible simulations for emerging business leaders, then use APAC family-business succession as the distinctive high-value pathway once the scoring model and user base have more evidence.

## Clean one-line positioning

Use this:

**Acumen helps emerging business leaders practise realistic decisions, understand their leadership tendencies, and connect with peers whose strengths complement their own.**

Avoid this:

**Acumen is an AI-powered platform for family-business successors with CV parsing, global maps, certificates, recruiter tools, and validated cognitive scoring.**

The second version overclaims the MVP and makes the concept feel confusing.

## What to mark as future roadmap

Keep these out of the core MVP story unless they are clearly labelled as future features:

- CV parsing and profile upload
- World map network visualisation
- Recruiter dashboard
- Paid institutional certificates
- Fully automated AI matching
- Validated psychometric scoring
- Star ratings or connection strength

## What the MVP already proves

The MVP is enough for the coursework if framed properly:

- It shows a clear user journey.
- It demonstrates the simulation mechanic.
- It gives visible feedback and score outputs.
- It links assessment to peer matching.
- It reflects the research themes of leadership readiness, succession preparation, judgement, and networks.
- It is honest about limitations.

## Interview and survey validation plan

If only two interviews are possible, call them **exploratory interviews**, not full validation.

Use this wording:

**Two exploratory interviews were used to test early assumptions about the problem and value proposition. These findings are directional rather than conclusive, so the report combines them with survey responses, secondary research, and prototype feedback. Broader validation remains a next step.**

What the two interviews should test:

1. Do users understand the product in under one minute?
2. Do they see simulation feedback as useful or generic?
3. Would they trust peer matching based on skills and decision style?
4. Does the APAC family-business angle feel compelling, too narrow, or better as an advanced pathway?
5. What would make the product feel credible enough to use again?

## Human wording for the report

Use:

**The research shows a real preparation gap, especially around succession, governance, and leadership readiness. The MVP does not solve succession directly. Instead, it tests a smaller and more achievable product idea: can short business simulations help emerging leaders practise judgement, understand themselves better, and form more useful peer connections?**

Use:

**The beachhead is deliberately broader than the research anchor. Students and young professionals are easier to reach and test with, while NextGen successors represent a higher-value later segment. This makes the concept more practical without losing the strategic link to APAC family-business succession.**

Avoid:

**The platform will transform succession through validated behavioural analytics and AI-driven network intelligence.**

That sounds bigger than the evidence.
