# Acumen Source Audit and Report Layout Guide

This file checks whether the research base is usable for the report and what is still needed to match the exemplar layout.

## 1. Overall Source Verdict

The research direction is valid.

The strongest evidence base is:
- APAC family-business succession and NextGen readiness.
- Indonesian family-business governance and senior resistance.
- Serious games and simulation-based soft skill assessment.
- Upper Echelons Theory and decision-making frameworks.
- Market feasibility through university and later institutional channels.

The weak point is not the secondary research. The weak point is primary validation. The final report needs at least a small survey and a few interviews to prove that students, young professionals, and aspiring founders actually want the product.

Use these in the main report with citations.

### APAC wealth transfer

Between 2023 and 2030, high-net-worth and ultra-high-net-worth families in Asia Pacific are expected to transfer around USD 5.8 trillion to the next generation.

Why the premium later segment, NextGen family business successors, matters.

Use for:
Evidence that NextGen family-business leadership is a documented research area.

### Largest family businesses

Claim:
EY and the University of St.Gallen's Global 500 Family Business Index shows that the largest family businesses represent a major global economic force.

Use for:
Showing that family businesses are economically significant.

Source:
EY and University of St.Gallen Global 500 Family Business Index.

### Serious games and soft skills

Claim:
Serious games have been used to assess or develop soft skills such as problem solving, teamwork, time management, decision-making, and communication.

Use for:
Justifying why a simulation is more than a gimmick.

JMIR Serious Games, "Gamification and Soft Skills Assessment in the Development of a Serious Game: Design and Feasibility Pilot Study", 2023.

### Political connections in Indonesia

Claim:

Fisman, R. (2001), "Estimating the Value of Political Connections", American Economic Review, 91(4), 1095-1102.

## 3. Claims That Need Careful Wording

### "Family companies in Indonesia contribute more than 80% of GDP"

Status:
Usable with care. The cleanest source found is Sukamdani (2023), a Journal of ASEAN Studies literature review, which states that family companies in Indonesia reach 40% of market capitalisation and contribute more than 80% of GDP. Because this is a literature review statement, not a new government data calculation, phrase it carefully.

Report wording:
"Sukamdani's (2023) review of Southeast Asian family-business literature states that Indonesian family companies reach around 40% of market capitalisation and contribute more than 80% of GDP, underlining why succession quality has wider economic significance."

Even safer wording:
"Family businesses are widely described as central to Indonesia's economy, with Sukamdani (2023) citing their major role in market capitalisation and GDP."

Source:
Sukamdani, N.B. (2023) "Family Business Dynamics in Southeast Asia: A Comparative Study of Indonesia, Malaysia, Singapore, and Thailand", Journal of ASEAN Studies, 11(1), pp. 197-218. DOI: 10.21512/jas.v11i1.9518.

### "Family businesses in Indonesia reach 40% of market capitalisation"

Status:
Usable with the same source and caveat as above.

Report wording:
"Sukamdani (2023) cites Indonesian family companies as reaching around 40% of market capitalisation."

Do not overuse this number. It should support the context, not carry the whole market argument.

### "McKinsey has a 25-person Game-Based Innovation Lab"

Status:
Usable. McKinsey's own article states that since late 2020 it has had a game-based innovation lab with about 25 colleagues creating digital games for recruiting, client learning academy, and professional development.

Report wording:
"McKinsey's own account of its Game-Based Innovation Lab shows that game-based assessment and learning are being used in serious professional contexts, not only entertainment."

Source:
McKinsey & Company (2025) "From apprenticeship in space to selecting microbes: Meet McKinsey's game-based innovation lab."

### "Imbellus scores were validated through McKinsey employees from 2017 onwards"

Status:
Partly usable, but the original wording is too strong. The better supported claim is that Imbellus and McKinsey piloted the game in November 2017 with around 500 candidates in London to collect feedback and calibrate the scoring engine. A 2018 Educational Data Mining paper reports a pilot of 527 McKinsey candidates and says the pilot data would inform future designs, validate methodologies, and refine scores.

Report wording:
"Imbellus and McKinsey provide a useful analogy for simulation-based assessment. Their 2017 pilot used candidate data to calibrate and refine scoring, but Acumen does not claim equivalent validation at MVP stage."

Avoid:
"Imbellus scores were validated on McKinsey employees from 2017 onwards."

Better:
"Imbellus piloted and calibrated simulation-based assessment with McKinsey candidates from 2017, showing the level of validation work required before such scores can be treated as high-stakes assessment evidence."

Sources:
McKinsey & Company (2020) "Innovating recruiting through online gaming."
Kantar, R. et al. (2018) "Constructing Cognitive Profiles for Simulation-Based Hiring Assessments", Educational Data Mining.

### "AI adoption is only 8% among APAC family businesses"

Status:
Likely from PwC NextGen 2024, but verify exact wording before final use.

Use only if the PwC page or PDF directly supports it.

## 5. What The Exemplar Layout Does

The exemplar report has this structure:

1. Cover page with venture name.
2. Assignment and word count page.
3. Table of contents.
4. Executive summary.
5. Scope.
6. Definition of the product.
7. Lean Business Model Canvas.
8. Market analysis.
9. Customer analysis and interview evidence.
10. Overall feasibility.
11. Appendix.
12. Bibliography.

The exemplar is visually heavy. It uses:
- A clear product identity.
- A table of contents.
- Figures and screenshots.
- A canvas diagram.
- Market maps.
- Customer journey visuals.
- Prototype screenshots.
- Interview evidence.
- Appendices for raw supporting material.
- A final feasibility judgement.

## 7. Recommended Main Report Layout

Use this instead of copying the exemplar exactly.

### Cover Page

Include:
- Acumen logo/name.
- "Business judgment simulations for emerging leaders."
- Module name.
- Group members.
- Word count.

### Table of Contents

Keep it clean and one page.

### 1. Executive Summary, 250 to 300 words

Include:
- The problem.
- The solution.
- Beachhead users.
- Premium later segment.
- Evidence base.
- MVP status.
- Feasibility verdict.

### 2. Scope and Feasibility Questions, 250 to 300 words

Explain:
- What the report tests.
- What is in scope.
- What is out of scope.
- The main uncertainty: desirability and trust, not basic technical feasibility.

Use 3 to 4 questions:
- Do emerging business leaders feel underprepared?
- Do simulations feel useful?
- Do users trust structured feedback?
- Does peer matching add value?

### 3. Product Definition and MVP, 350 to 450 words

Include:
- Product definition.
- Product loop diagram.
- MVP screenshot collage.
- Current features.
- Out-of-scope features as deliberate post-MVP extensions.

### 4. Research Context, 500 to 650 words

Include:
- Serious games and soft skill assessment.
- APAC family-business succession pressure.
- Indonesian governance and succession context.
- Why this research anchor gives Acumen a serious scenario engine.

Do not let this section become only family-business history. Always connect it back to the product.

### 5. Customer Validation, 400 to 500 words

Include:
- Survey sample.
- Key findings.
- Interview insights.
- What changed in the MVP or positioning because of feedback.

If primary research is still light, say so clearly and use it as a limitation.

### 6. Business Model and Value Proposition, 350 to 450 words

Include:
- BMC visual.
- VPC visual.
- Short explanation of revenue logic.
- Why universities are the first channel.
- Why NextGen successors are later premium users.

### 7. Market Attractiveness and Competition, 450 to 600 words

Use:
- SWOT 2x2 table.
- Porter's Five Forces compact table.
- 150 to 250 words explaining the main implication.

Do not use long SWOT or Porter essays in the main body.

### 8. Feasibility Verdict and Next Steps, 350 to 450 words

Include:
- Is Acumen feasible now?
- What is the biggest risk?
- What evidence is still needed?
- SMART next steps.

## 8. SWOT And Porter Format Decision

Use tables or diagrams, not long text.

Main report:
- SWOT: 2x2 table, 3 bullets per quadrant.
- Porter: five-force table with rating and implication.

Appendix:
- Longer explanation if needed.

Reason:
The exemplar uses visual tools heavily. It does not bury frameworks in long blocks. The marking criteria also reward clear presentation, so concise diagrams will score better than dense prose.

## 9. Research Fit Check

The research now fits the concept if framed like this:

Acumen is not "an app for heirs only." It is a business judgment practice platform for emerging leaders.

The APAC family-business research is used because:
- It gives the simulations a real high-stakes context.
- It shows why leadership readiness matters economically.
- It makes Acumen more distinctive than a generic leadership app.
- It supports the later premium segment.

The survey/interview research should test:
- Whether students and young professionals want decision practice.
- Whether simulation feedback feels useful and credible.
- Whether peer matching based on strengths feels valuable.
- Whether the family-business scenario context feels serious rather than too niche.

## 10. Source Risk Checklist

Before final submission:
- Every statistic needs a citation.
- Every quote needs page/source detail where possible.
- Remove unsupported numbers if the citation is weak.
- Do not cite ChatGPT-generated URLs or links with tracking parameters.
- Prefer PwC, McKinsey, EY, academic journals, and original papers.
- Use "suggests" or "indicates" for interpretive claims.
- Do not claim the scoring is validated. Say it is research-informed and needs calibration.

To reach exemplar standard, create:
- Cover page.

The report should look visual, not text-heavy.
