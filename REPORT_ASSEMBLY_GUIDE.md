# Acumen report assembly guide

This is the older practical guide for turning the current materials into the final report. For the one-file version, use `ACUMEN_MASTER_REPORT_HANDOFF.md`.

## Current report verdict

The project is cohesive enough to start writing.

The strongest version is:

**Acumen helps emerging business leaders practise realistic decisions, understand their leadership tendencies, and connect with peers whose strengths complement their own.**

The report should then explain:

- **Beachhead:** business students, young professionals, and aspiring founders.
- **Premium later segment:** NextGen family-business successors.
- **Research anchor:** APAC family-business succession, governance, and leadership readiness.
- **MVP proof:** simulation, feedback, profile, and peer matching.
- **Honest limitation:** scores are research-informed, not yet externally validated.

This gives the marker a clean line from research to product to feasibility.

## Suggested report structure

Use this order:

1. **Executive summary**
   State the problem, solution, target market, MVP, and feasibility verdict.

2. **Scope and opportunity**
   Explain the leadership-readiness problem, then connect it to APAC succession as the deeper research anchor.

3. **Product definition**
   Define Acumen in one sentence. Add the product loop diagram.

4. **MVP walkthrough**
   Use the screenshot sequence, not a collage. Show the product as a story.

5. **Business model and value proposition**
   Insert the BMC and VPC. Explain that the BMC is the beachhead and the VPC shows the premium later segment.

6. **Market validation**
   Use survey results, two exploratory interviews, and secondary research. Be honest about limits.

7. **Market attractiveness**
   Add SWOT and Porter's Five Forces as concise tables or diagrams.

8. **Feasibility and next steps**
   Say the MVP is feasible for coursework and early testing, while validation and scoring calibration remain future work.

9. **Appendix**
   Put raw interview notes, survey details, extra screenshots, BMC, VPC, and supporting research.

10. **Bibliography**
   Use the source audit file.

## Wording to use for sources

Use:

**Sukamdani's (2023) review of Southeast Asian family-business literature states that Indonesian family companies reach around 40% of market capitalisation and contribute more than 80% of GDP.**

Use:

**McKinsey's Game-Based Innovation Lab shows that simulation-based assessment and learning are used in serious professional contexts, not only entertainment.**

Use:

**Imbellus and McKinsey piloted and calibrated simulation-based assessment with McKinsey candidates from 2017, but Acumen does not claim equivalent validation at MVP stage.**

Avoid:

**Imbellus scores were validated on McKinsey employees from 2017 onwards.**

## Does the work meet the marking direction?

Yes, if the report is written honestly and visually.

The project now has:

- A named product.
- A clear user segment.
- A research-backed market context.
- A usable MVP.
- A product loop.
- A BMC and VPC.
- A source audit.
- Screenshot evidence.
- SWOT and Porter material.
- A clear limitation story.

The highest-risk area is still primary validation. Two interviews can work if they are framed as exploratory. The report should not pretend they prove demand. It should say they informed early assumptions alongside survey data and secondary research.

## Final writing rule

Keep the main report concise, visual, and confident.

Do not make the report sound like Acumen has already built every future feature. The top-mark version is not the biggest version. It is the most coherent version.
